Pioneer settler history of migration, settlement and adaptation to Canadian society

 

Canadian immigration changes allowed South Asian Muslims to migrate to B.C., fostering community organizations and mosques despite racism.

 

Post-9/11, South Asian Muslims in B.C. faced discrimination and surveillance, with emerging voices and solidarity with Indigenous groups.
